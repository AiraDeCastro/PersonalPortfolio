Lipa City, Batangas

Sometimes called the "Little Rome of the Philippines" for its major religious and historic sites. Its stunning sites and attractions make it a paradise for pilgrims and tourists. It is one of Batangas' three major cities. It was also once known as the world's coffee capital.

It is one of the three cities in the region and lies 78 kilometers south of Manila, along with Tanauan and Batangas City. It is named after the lipa (Dendrocnide meyeniana), a type of tree in the Urticaceae family that is well-known for having stinging trichomes on its twigs.
